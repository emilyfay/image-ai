Image Completion with Deep Learning

A deep convolutional neural network was trained to infill masked regions in images using a generative adversarial network (DCGAN).

The network was trained on over 120,000 64 x 64 pixel head shots of people (from the LFW and CACD databases). The training involved two adversarial networks: a generator and a discriminator. The generator takes in an image with a masked region and outputs a new image. The discriminator takes in both original images and the generated images and classifies them as real or fake. The discriminator is trained to get better at differentiating between the real images and those from the generator, while the generator is trained to produce images that can fool the discriminator. Together, the networks can learn to infill images with realistic-looking content.

In order to train a GAN, both networks must continue to learn at a similar rate. Using thresholds, learning could be paused for a network when it got too far ahead of the other.


The code is written in Tensorflow, using the tutorial by Brandon Amos as a starting point. His code is based on the paper "Semantic Image Inpainting with Perceptual and Contextual Losses" by Raymond Yeh et al. The method used here to infill images is modeled after the generator described by C. Sauer et al. with some slight modifications.

Generator architecture:
[64 channel 5x5 CONV stride 1 + leakyReLU + batchnorm] x 5
[64 channel 3x3 CONV stride 1 + leakyReLU + batchnorm]
[tanh]

Discriminator architecture:
[64 channel 5x5 CONV stride 2 + leakyReLU]
[128 channel 5x5 CONV stride 2 + leakyReLU]
[256 channel 5x5 CONV stride 2 + leakyReLU]
[512 channel 5x5 CONV stride 2 + leakyReLU]
[fully connected layer]
[sigmoid]
